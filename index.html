Hello, my name is Ryan Orendorff. I am currently a nsf grfp funded PhD candidate in the Joint Bioengineering program at UC Berkeley/UCSF, working in the Berkeley Imaging Systems Laboratory. I work on novel image reconstruction methods for magnetic particle imaging (mpi) using convex optimization techniques. On the side, I do software consulting with the Innolitics medical imaging software consulting firm.

I spend most of my free time investigating new programming languages and type theory as these methods provide safety guarantees that a program will run as expected. The intersection of type theory and optimization is particularly potent; the optimization can guarantee a certain set of results will happen, while type theory allows one to express the mathematical model in a provably correct way in a computational setting.

Previously I have worked at the following places.

  • At the Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratory (merl), I developed new algorithms for efficient model predictive control on constrained systems.
  • At the National Center for Atmospheric Research (ncar), I built a custom laser hygrometer to measure water concentration on aircraft platforms such as a C-130 and a Gulfstream 5 jet. In this work I both designed and built the optics as well as the software stack used to control the device. I went on a three week field project in St. Croix to measure the effects of small particulates on cloud formation in the tropics (the ICE-T project).
I have had the pleasure of attending the following universities.
  • Cambridge University. I was awarded a Cambridge Overseas Trust Scholarship to obtained a Masters of Philosophy by Research in Chemical Engineering and Biotechnology in the Laser Analytics Group. During my time, I investigated novel broadband cavity absorption spectroscopy designs for detecting europium in aqueous solutions. My thesis can be found here: pdf, code.
  • Tufts University. At Tufts I pioneered new ways to use silk as an optical medium in the Omenetto lab. For my honors thesis I created a software framework for using the Microsoft Kinect as a platform for at home monitoring of patients after cardiac surgeries (pdf).
